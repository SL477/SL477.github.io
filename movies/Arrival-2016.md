This movie raised some interesting philosophical questions, if you know the future should you follow it.
And that is as far as I will go on what the main question is.
The aliens were able to see through time.

Aliens are hard in science fiction, most are effectively a human/earth animal who speaks English.
Even in written fiction generally we are dealing with first contact being solved (or it's aliens turn up looking for a fight)[^2].

The original Stargate movie is the closest equivalent that I can think of.
Daniel Jackson must learn how to communicate with the humans on the planet Abydos and learns Goa'uld[^1].

In Arrival the aliens are a lot more alien, squid-like insecty things.
Their language is ink circles (spoiler) that they spit out, the noises they make have no bearing on the language.
In a lot of science fiction the aliens are generally parallels for different groups, and it's hard for audiences to empathize with a giant blurry squid (and much cheaper & easier to slap green paint on an actor and call it a day)[^3].

[^1]: Effectively ancient Egyptian. In the subsequent TV series everyone speaks English more or less.

[^2]: I get it, you can't subject audiences to learning made up languages in every film.

[^3]: much more satisfactory than the planet of the hats trope.
