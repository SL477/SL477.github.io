[My reply to the IndieWeb Carnival prompt from Zachary Kai](https://zacharykai.net/notes/icfeb26). 2026-02-01.
My main interest I suppose is in creating things. I am a programmer by profession[^1], my model railway is currently on hold while I move[^2] and a lot of Warhammer 40k and Trench Crusade miniatures have gone under my brush.

I also enjoy building up more of my website, Lego and cooking. On the less practical side I enjoy computer games and reading (crime, science fiction and history). One of my longer term interests is writing.

On my model railway, originally set in the vague age of steam, has some Legiones Imperialis tanks built for it (and a thunderhawk gunship which it hasn't met). Which definitely is a bit of an intersection with the world of Warhammer 40k[^3]. 

My Trench Crusade figures have made it to my website, or at least the React part of it. They made a good theme for the Odin Project stuff I was doing. I finally finished my Space Marines of many legions, next up is Kill Team.

My Lego collection has mostly paused now while I move. I did change my Lego Christmas tree from two trees to one over Christmas.

On my website, I've been trying to get a load of stuff done for it. There is always more!

On the reading front I have read quite a few of the Warhammer 40k books. Also I have been getting through a lot of the Agatha Christie books. I did also read a book on the Arctic Convoys, which one of my Grandfathers was part of and a book on the RAF Squadron my other grandfather was in. There is a lot of World War 2 history that is only briefly covered in documentaries.

I have been very slowly writing a book. Most of the evidence are the shorter bits of fiction I dump on my website. One day it will be done!

[^1]: Eventually, I started out as an accountant.

[^2]: Some would suggest it had stalled a while ago.

[^3]: There is a 10k time gap, but then some of the tanks are from Epic 40k. So that gives you some idea of how much I care for realism/purity.
