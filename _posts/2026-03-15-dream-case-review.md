[My submission for the IndieWeb prompt: we've got to dream past it](https://zacharykai.net/notes/ifcfeb26).

Reverend Arthur Peachy heard the letter drop into his virtual inbox as he made his way to bed. He skimmed its contents quickly in his peripheral vision as he brushed his teeth.

*Dear Reverend Peachy,*

*I write to tell you that the wrong man was convicted of the murder of my grandmother Agatha Blanche. My father Colin is a gentle soul and wouldn't hurt anyone. At the moment he is on death row at Hastings Orbital. I attach all relevant evidence. Please find who did this.*

*Yours in hope*

*Bridget Blanche*

Arthur Peachy lay down in bed and fully activated his neural implants.

He appeared inside his own head. Or at least that was the general impression, as he was now asleep and a copy of his mind was running in his computer. The room had rounded white walls and featured a large computer console at the front, with two large screens where eyes would be (currently black). A small kitchen area more reminiscent of that in an office, was setup in the area to the right eye. A small library area in the left. A pair of staircases led up to a balcony area and corridors where the ears would be. A grand oak table stood between them, a chess board lay with his postal game. At the rear was a lift and around the table were a few sofas.

Peachy spread out the letter in front of him and started looking through the case. There did not appear to be any more evidence against Colin than anyone else.
He decided that there was just enough to go on and alerted Jonathan and Zari. The simulation was running fast enough that it would be a while before they appeared in it. Arthur sent a few searches off about the case, it was something of a long shot as he rather suspected that Hastings Orbital was in a different star system given that he had never heard of it.
On a whim he started downloading a simulation of Hastings Orbital.
As he waited he looked at his postal chess letter, took the move and wrote out his reply, he dropped it in his outbox and fired it off.

The lift dinged as Jonathan and Zari appeared, Arthur re-established the simulation's time stream. 
"Welcome both, we have a new case." Arthur announced to them. Jonathan his curate had decided to just accept his extra detective responsibilities. Zari his niece, parish secretary and bodyguard, was more enthusiastic about the ending of a case. A not so subtle glance at his boxes of cold cases was her idea of a reprimand (cold was hardly the word Peachy would use to describe them, awaiting more evidence was his term). Zari's more nuclear reprimanded was his sermon, but as it was Monday Peachy was safe for now.
"I take it that this is more of a consultation, given that we're asleep." Jonathan replied.
"What happened?" Zari signed, her translation disc repeating out loud.
"Agatha Blanche, stabbed to death on a train. In front of three or four people, depending on whether you count her murderer. None of them saw it happen, they believe it happened in the dark when the train went into a tunnel. Her son Colin was convicted of her murder, but his daughter believes him to be innocent." Peachy explained.
"Anything to suggest he wasn't guilty?" Jonathan asked.
"From the looks of the case against him, it was mostly centred on an argument the previous night between Colin and his mother, where he was heard to threaten killing her. There does not appear to be any more evidence against him than any of the others. They were in a seating compartment of the train, the steward had been in while Agatha Blanche was alive and came back in the commotion when it was discovered that she was dead." Peachy said, he unveiled the beginnings of the case board.

## The board
Victim: Agatha Blanche. 82, rich widow.

Cause of death: single stab wound to the heart

Weapon: flick knife, no fingerprints

### Suspects

#### Colin Blanche
Age: 46

Relationship to victim: son

Motive: money and heard arguing the night before and threatened to kill her.

#### Darren Blanche
Age: 44

Relationship to victim: son

Motive: money

#### Chiara Blanche
Age: 37

Relationship to victim: daughter

Motive: money

#### Simona Blanche
Age: 39

Relationship to victim: daughter in law (Colin's wife)

Motive: money

"Who's to say that the murderer has not already been found?" Jonathan asked.
"I have requested the full court files, for now we are double checking. If we find enough evidence against anyone else or cannot find anything to vindicate Colin, then we can call it a day." Peachy said, ignoring the incredulous look from Zari. "For now how about a train ride?" Knowing that that would be the fastest way to get Jonathan on side.
The three of them went into the lift. The doors opened onto Battle Station and vanished behind them. In front of them stood an ornate steam engine.
Steam billowed down the platform, passengers clambered aboard and doors slammed.
"All aboard!" Yelled a uniformed conductor. Peachy headed off to the train, at two metres tall he was hard to miss, and his red robotic arm made him more distinct. His hair was brown turning to grey. He wore a black suit with a dog collar.
Jonathan was thinner than Peachy, and a more respectable average height. His sandy hair was slowly migrating from the top of his head to his face, as Mrs Jones the church warden had uncharitably remarked. He was wearing a more casual blue shirt and black trousers.
At under one and a half metres Zari was the smallest of the trio. She was thinner than Jonathan, but not by much and a lot of it was muscle. Her long blonde hair was tied in a bun and she wore a bright blue scarf over her lurid yellow t-shirt, with dark green cargo trousers. She had joined the Anglian Militia like Peachy had, the 13th Absolution Corps as opposed to the 1st Marine Division. The Absolution Corps were penal divisions, apart from the 13th, who were volunteers from Telos Prime (meaning descendants of genetically engineered super-soldiers).
The three of them made it onto the train, which logically couldn't leave without them anyway.
Jonathan followed Arthur down the train, on the basis that he looked like he knew where he was going. He checked behind him to see if Zari was following and saw that she was busy checking if she was being followed too.
"This is us," Arthur announced entering a compartment, with the other two close behind. Like the rest of the train, the compartment had wooden panelling to keep the rustic feel. Hastings Orbital had something of an anti-technology sentiment, despite being a wholly artificial planetoid. There were windows looking into the corridor.
"How accurate is this to the location?" Jonathan asked.
"It's the right sort of train and the right sort of carriage. We have the witness statements. Only the family members and victim are accurate, everyone else you see is made up by the simulation." Peachy replied.
An old woman appeared sitting in the middle of the seats where Peachy was sitting.
"Agatha Blanche, I presume," Jonathan said.
"A pleasure," she said, her voice sounding like she had just bitten into something unpleasant. Her hat sat grandly on her head, it was something of a work of art, a big bow built on top of it. A pair of pince-nez glasses sat on her nose. She was rather rotund and had a large coat and woollen skirt on.
"Before we get into the case notes I want to find out how none of the witnesses saw who stabbed her." Peachy said. "There were four people surrounding her and none of them saw the murder itself. Simona Blanche was reading her book and sitting opposite Agatha by the door, where Jonathan is. She was the first to notice the knife and raise the alarm." A flick-knife appeared in Peachy's hand. "The murder weapon. Jonathan, you can be Simona as you're sitting in the right place, now try murdering Agatha."
"I don't want to be Simona, or murder someone," Jonathan replied. Zari rolled her eyes and swapped seats with Jonathan.
"Well volunteered," Peachy said and passed her the knife.
"I'm not killing anyone either," Zari said through her translator.
"Your war record..." Jonathan started and then tailed off as he saw Arthur violently shaking his head and waving his hands to stop him.
"The Inheritors are aliens and non-sentient ones. They don't count. I've never killed anyone." Zari signed angrily. Arthur handed her a foam knife and took away the metal one.
"On a happier topic do you want to dress up as Simona?" Peachy asked.
"Sure," she said. "But I keep the scarf."
"Naturally, I wouldn't dream of depriving you of it." Arthur replied. Suddenly Zari was wearing a grey skirt and white blouse, with a matching grey jacket. A book of poetry appeared on her lap, a pair of glasses on her face and a red beret hat on her head. "It doesn't say what book she had, so feel free to change it." Darren Blanche appeared on the opposite side of Agatha, so that he sat by the window.
"Would anyone really kill like this?" Jonathan asked. "Three witnesses in the compartment. Anyone able to see what occurred from the corridor." He indicated the windows in the door to the corridor and at the top of the walls to both sides of it. "Not to mention anyone able to see in from the outside."
"For now we assume they did kill her like this. For the outside, look at where we are, the middle of nowhere." Arthur said, indicating the open countryside out of the window. "This is approximately the time of the murder. And there isn't that much apart from farmland for the next few minutes. For potential witnesses," the room went dark for thirty seconds as the train went through a tunnel. "There's a slightly longer tunnel in a minute. That deals with witnesses in here too."
The room went black again.
"Oh, I take it that I'm murdered?" Agatha Blanche said questioningly and somehow condescendingly too. She said it almost the moment it had gone dark.
When the carriage made it out of the tunnel Jonathan saw that Agatha now had the foam knife in her lap.
"Oh no a murder," Zari's translator said completely expressionlessly as she put down her book. She appeared not to have moved.
"Now you can play Chiara Blanche. This time pretend that you're an amateur assassin who has to move at human speed." Arthur said, exercising his patience by not sounding too exasperated.
Arthur and Zari swapped seats, Zari's outfit transforming into the white flower print cotton dress that Chiara had been wearing. Zari looked down with an expression of deep disgust at the pattern and picked at the uncomfortable material of the dress.
"She's definitely the killer, no one in their right mind would wear this." She announced and grabbed the real knife off of Peachy.
"Try to act casual, Chiara wasn't sitting there poised with a knife ready to murder her mother." Reluctantly Zari sat on the knife. Peachy flicked through the statements. "She was doing some sort of embroidery work at the time."
Zari snorted with laughter at that and the compartment went dark at the same time. "Actually... Oh," Agatha started saying and slumped dead. Her last oh being more the sound of air forced from her lungs when Zari stabbed her through the heart.
"You could have waited until she stopped talking." Arthur chided. Zari had transformed back into her original outfit.
"We did however hear the moment of death," Jonathan said. "So we did learn something."
Peachy inspected the knife in Agatha's chest. "Well placed, you used your scarf to wipe any fingerprints. But I guess Chiara had her embroidery pattern, and Simona had her jacket." He turned to Jonathan. "Now you get to play Darren." Jonathan noticed that Zari's fangs were extended and she was staring in a mixture of horror and hunger at Agatha. Agatha woke up and pulled the knife out of her chest.
"I believe you will need this," she said and handed it to Jonathan. Her knife wound had disappeared.
"Zari, go find the buffet car and calm down. We'll join you shortly." Peachy ordered. Zari shot Agatha a poisonous look, with her vampire fangs fully extended it was quite effective and left.
Jonathan moved to the seat that Darren had vacated by vanishing. His clothes suddenly shifted to match the yellow checked waistcoat, and pinstripe suit that Darren had been wearing. "If it's OK Arthur I'd like the foam knife." Arthur rolled his eyes and handed him the foam knife too.
"OK, Zari is out of the coach and I've locked us in. I'm splitting the train's timeline in two so that resets here don't affect where Zari is." Colin appeared opposite Jonathan and Chiara opposite Arthur. "Anyway, in your own time."
The compartment went dark, Jonathan sorted out the hiding place for his foam knife while the train was in the first tunnel. He attempted to look inconspicuous during the spell in daylight. When it went dark again Jonathan slipped the foam knife out and rather self-consciously and gently stabbed Agatha with it.
"I wouldn't quit your day job, you would be on your way to prison by now as an assassin." Arthur criticised.
"I'm more than happy to watch you try to murder her. Or for you to bring Zari back." Jonathan snapped back and handed over the foam knife.
Peachy concealed the knife in his jacket and took Colin's seat. He managed to look nonchalant during the daylight stretch. Then when the compartment went black he struck. "Ow," he yelped as he hit his head on the luggage rack above the seats.
"Ouch," yelled Agatha as Peachy trod on her foot. He tapped her chest with the knife anyway.
"Smooth work Reverend," Jonathan said, trying not to sound too snarky.
"It's been a while since basic training," Arthur complained. "And I was never good at mêlée combat. That's what Zari's lot were for."

After a more successful second attempt they called it a day to reconstruction and headed to the back of the train to the buffet car.
The rear of the buffet car was one big window, where you could see where the train had been. Zari had taken over the seats at the back, on the grounds that she was the only real person in the car. She had her sketchbook out on the table and, as she had her back to the window, she was sketching the rest of the coach. This was mostly so that she didn't have to spend as much time looking behind her for potential murderers.
"I see the deductions are going well then." Arthur said, sitting down opposite her. Jonathan ordered a round of drinks before joining them.
"I believe it's time to look for more motives." Arthur said as Jonathan sat down.
"Your files and drinks sir," said the waiter, depositing a tray of coffees and a pile of files on the table. They had everything from the court case and some background on all of the victims.
"Does it say why they were on the train?" Jonathan asked.
The waiter sat down next to Zari. "They were on their way back from holidaying a quarter of the way around the habitat from their house. This train eventually goes all the way around the ring, which is why it doesn't stop that often."
Half an hour later they went through another couple of tunnels and arrived at Hollyhock Station. There the train was watered and fuelled (with wood grown on the habitat) and passengers got on and off. As they had another half hour of waiting at the station both Jonathan and Zari took a walk down the platform, Jonathan admired the steam engine at the front of the train. Guilt eventually dragged Jonathan back to the buffet car and the stack of reading material, Zari got back to her drawing and ignoring her stack of papers.
The pile of papers seemed to go on forever as the train chugged its way around the habitat. The track ran more or less dead straight down the centre of the habitat, so only the scenery behind them changed. The city of Hollyhock gave way to smaller towns, then back to farmland and then through a forest and then up and down through a mountain range. Another station stop beckoned. This was a skiing stop, but another round of fuelling happened, there had been brief halts on the way to the mountain stop.
Jonathan felt as though his simulated eyeballs were going to fall out. His pile of papers had been gone through. He rather dreaded the idea of going through the ones that Zari and Arthur had read too.
"Time for something to eat I think," Arthur announced much to Jonathan's relief. The three of them strolled down the platform and re-boarded the train at the dining car.
"A pleasure to meet you all, follow me to your table." The waiter said and guided them to one of the tables and took their orders. With much whistling and door slamming the train moved off from the station. As it was now getting dark the train staff were lighting oil lamps throughout the train. Earth may have more or less run out of oil, but algae on the habitat had been engineered to produce it and it was one of Hastings Orbital's main exports.
"What have we learnt today?" Arthur asked.
"Steam trains stink," Zari answered.
"That the killer must have had nerves of steel and subtly." Jonathan answered, deciding that that was the topic that Arthur wanted to discuss.
"They have a big house by the sea," Zari put in. The waiter reappeared and deposited their drinks.
"The prosecution's theory was that Simona caused a distraction and then Colin struck. They didn't try her as an accomplice, so obviously they had less evidence against her than Colin." Arthur stated. The train rattled its descent down the mountainside as the waiter deposited their food on the table.
"Weak theory," Zari said, her translator robbing her voice of the somewhat dismissive way she had signed it. "If either caused a distraction for the other, then they caused either Darren or Chiara to look at Agatha."
"Yes, I guess that they could have caused a distraction that got them away from Agatha. But that is the problem with a distraction theory in this case. About the only combo it might work in are Darren or Chiara causing a distraction to get Simona and Colin to look away. But they also might see the murder." Arthur replied. They were a bit distracted themselves by food.
"I think that this case may come down to motive," Jonathan announced suddenly, surprising himself. Arthur looked at him expectantly, Zari was watching the window. "All four had opportunity, they were at least in the compartment with the victim and may or may not have worked out a master plan to commit the crime. The means was a flick knife, a nasty bit of kit, but not something that would be too difficult to get your hands on. And unless one of them was found with a knife insurance policy or receipt for a knife, then that won't be much use." He petered out and took a sip of wine.
"I agree, hopefully there is a great big motive or we have missed something in the opportunity." Arthur replied.
"What did they all do?" Zari asked.
"The Blanche family owned one of the algae plants," Arthur answered. "Agatha was effectively retired, but the majority shareholder. Colin was the CEO of the company. Chiara Chief Operating Officer and Darren Chief Financial Officer. I'm not sure about Simona."
"Some sort of PR job at another company." Jonathan put in.
There was no more shop talk, after dessert one of their fellow passengers was recruited to make a fourth for bridge. The train stopped briefly on the other side of the mountain range for water and started crossing the next section of the habitat, this was mostly desert and almost entirely wasted on the passengers who couldn't see that much of it in the dark. At the next station all they could see were hints of dunes in the distance beyond the town. The far side of the habitat was currently being lit. The habitat experienced a twenty four hour day-night cycle, but that bore almost no relation to its spin cycle.
The three of them were assigned cabins in one of the sleeper cars in first class. The cabins were not overly generous in terms of space. This made Arthur wonder about what second class got. He attempted reading his book and soon gave up and turned the oil lamp off.

Arthur woke up early, the train's rocking now making him feel ill. He got up and walked through the train towards the front. There was a corridor that ran under the tender so that he could get right up to the locomotive. Naturally Jonathan was there, he had had a go at being a fireman and an engine driver and was now having a rest.
"Morning Arthur," he said. The driver and fireman grunted their acknowledgement of him.
"Morning Jonathan, that's an alarming amount of water ahead of us." Arthur said, looking through the forward windows. It was a somewhat novel experience being able to see ahead. The train had many views to the side and a panoramic view of where they'd been. But just two tiny windows for what was ahead.
"We are almost at the end of the line." Jonathan announced.
"What!?" Arthur exclaimed. "But the line goes all the way around the ring."
"It goes almost the entire way around the habitat. For thirty kilometres you take a steamer across the sea. The other twenty thousand kilometres the train can do." Jonathan explained.
"You have a choice to either take the steamer to the other railhead or take this train back the way we came." The driver said, the computer wanting a decision for what to load next.
The driver gave a blast on the whistle as they pulled into the station. Arthur and Jonathan headed back up into the train and exited from the first carriage.
Jonathan hurried off to watch the locomotive detach and go through the turntable. Zari joined Arthur on the platform. They had an hour if they didn't fiddle with the simulation. The buffet car would be detached and spun around, the passengers herded to the jetty and the next wave loaded on. The two of them headed round to the beach. The steamer was on its way into the harbour and blocked a lot of the view. With the curve of the habitat they could see the other side of the sea extending up above them, they could just see the town on the other side.
"This would have been the Blanches' last stop," Arthur announced. "They live down the coast on the far side of the sea."
Jonathan appeared just then bearing ice creams. "Have a pre-breakfast snack. We've got to make the most of not being in reality."
He went to pass Arthur his ice cream and dropped it on the sand. "Sorry, my mistake." He said, noticing that Zari was busy checking for murderers behind her, he handed Arthur what would have been hers.
"Could it be that simple?" Peachy asked rhetorically and started heading back to the train. Jonathan and Zari headed after him.
The small diesel engine at the station was manoeuvring the buffet car down to the turntable so that it could face the rear. The locomotive was in a small siding being refuelled and watered.

They caught up with Peachy in the dining car. He had a small model of the train setup on a small loop of track. A couple of small tunnels and station were on it. His ice-cream lay on a saucer abandoned.
Jonathan and Zari had the presence of mind to order breakfast from the waiter before rejoining Arthur.
"If the murder happened here, at Hollyhock Station, or rather the tunnels just before that. Then the murderer could potentially have got away before the alarm was raised. And given that the town is above one of the habitat's spaceports, they could have got very far away indeed. It would have been an awful gamble as to whether the murder was noticed before her killer was away. Or if people had boarded at the station into the compartment, then it would have been unclear as to whether Mrs Blanche had been killed by a complete stranger." Arthur enthused. Rolling the train around the track dramatically for effect. "Instead the murderer kills her one set of tunnels too soon and the murder is noticed almost immediately."
"Good theory, any way to prove it?" Jonathan asked.
Arthur gave an evil smirk and the pile of papers reappeared on the table. "Glad you asked. If it's here then it is in there somewhere."
"It implies that the motive was on the train." Zari added.
"Yes, or the murderer would have had a better plan." Arthur replied.
Arthur did grudgingly allow them to have breakfast before they properly got into reading again. 
Added to the pile was the news from Hastings Orbital for the days before and on the murder, which Jonathan had seized. He sat down with his coffee to go through it. 
"Did you kill her?" Zari snapped at Simona, who had appeared opposite her at the dining table.
"Reset witness," Arthur barked, the shocked expression left Simona's face. "Keep your questioning to background information. Computer, you may paraphrase Simona's witness statements and court testimony. But do not deviate from its meaning and if you cannot find anything in her testimony then say that you cannot answer. Don't improvise."
"There was an argument in the dining car the night before the murder. What was it about?" Zari signed, deciding to play good cop this time.
Simona blinked as the computer with its new restrictions for her thought about it. "My mother in law had some ideas for how to run the company." She replied, simultaneously signing her response which was not something the real Simona would be able to do (technically everyone on the orbital would be speaking a different language too, so it was not too much of an immersion break. And also there was nothing in her background that said that Simona could not speak sign language).
"What sort of suggestions?" Zari signed, after out starring Simona didn't work.
"I'm sorry, that's not in my dataset." She replied.
"Next witness, same question," Zari snapped.
Simona vanished and Colin appeared in her place. "My mother saw fit to remove me as CEO of Blanche Chemicals." Zari gave him an evil eye. "She accused me of financial irregularities in the company."
"Was there any evidence to back that up?" Arthur interjected and grabbed one of the folders.
"Not that she provided to me. But apparently there was some." Colin replied.
"Any further information?" Arthur pressed.
"She didn't like my management style. I'm completely incompetent and not as good as my father." Colin summarised.
"Chiara next please," Arthur asked. Colin vanished and Chiara took his place. "Please tell us what the argument the night before the murder was about?"
"Mother gave one of her usual tirades about how useless we all were. I didn't pay much attention until she announced that she was taking over as CEO. Can't say that I was looking forward to it." Chiara said.
"The computer is obviously taking pity on you," Jonathan said. "I read her full statement and she went on a very long tangent about how great her department was."
"Darren please and same question." Arthur ordered.
"Mother and Colin had one of there regular ding dongs. Chiara and I knew to keep well out of it. Otherwise one of us might have been made CEO and have to deal with her interfering all the time." Darren concluded.
"That's roughly what he said in his witness statement." Arthur said.
"Interesting argument, it looks like that puts Darren into the frame as well as Colin. As financial irregularities certainly put the CFO in the frame." Jonathan started.
"Computer, could you find out more about the financial irregularities at Blanche Chemicals?" Arthur asked.

Arthur dragged them back into the buffet car. As the three of them got back to reading through more of the case the train's surroundings gradually got more arid as it moved back into the desert.
A whole selection of financial statements from Blanche Chemicals were now in the reading pile, just on the off chance that it was in the public data.
"It looks like the Interstellar *Iceshaker 3* was docked below Hollyhock." Jonathan announced, reading through the news.
"Not bad as escape plans go," Arthur commented. He and Zari were going through the physical statements, the computer was also having a look through them too.
"It seems that the Blanches' were paying through the nose for new algae." Arthur said, having received the computer's notes. "But we would likely need more evidence from Hastings Orbital."

The train stopped at a water and wood halt. It was at the edge of a usefully placed oasis. Jonathan and Arthur had a brief walk around the edge of the oasis. By exploiting the fact that they were in a simulation they came back bearing a coconut.
With a blast of its whistle the train started off again. The three of them had regathered in the murder compartment, the waiter dropped off a plate of sandwiches, a glass of coconut milk and slices of coconut.
"OK Arthur, have we got enough?" Jonathan asked.
"Enough to suggest reasonable doubt and a new theory for the police to investigate." Arthur replied. "I think it was Darren Blanche to try to cover up or escape the consequences of his fraud. If he had committed the murder at Hollyhock then he would have had a much better chance at escaping."
Arthur wrote the letter and enclosed their notes. They had a last day and night on the train.

The letter transferred out of the simulation, travelled down fibre-optic cables to a transmitter and was uploaded into space. The message dispatch network forwarded it onto a corporate ship on its way to Epsilon Eridani. Three days later the ship had arrived, there the letter was downloaded to the space station there. The next day it was sent to a passing cruise ship. Six stops and five weeks later it was passed to a cargo ship. The cargo ship upon arrival at Hastings Orbital transmitted that message and many others.

Three months later Arthur received another letter.

*Dear Reverend Peachy *

*Thank you for your letter. My father Colin has been released. It turned out that my uncle Darren had been stealing money from the company.*

*My grandmother realised that one of the companies was named after his childhood teddy and so discovered the fraud. Uncle Darren got caught after accessing the money and confessed.*

*Yours sincerely*

*Bridget Blanche*

Location: in front of victim, to the right by the door to the corridor.

Job: PR Advisor, Randall and Co

### Theory
Wrong location for the murder. The murderer was hoping to escape at Hollyhock Station and intended to kill the victim in the tunnels beforehand and escape in the confusion.

Potential financial irregularities at Blanche Chemicals indicative of theft.
