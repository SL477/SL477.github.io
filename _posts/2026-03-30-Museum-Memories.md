The Natural History Museum in London is my favourite museum.
As a lifelong lover of dinosaurs (walking with dinosaurs has a lot to answer for[^2]) it was brilliant looking at good old Dippy the Diplodocus[^1]. 
Seeing all of the old skeletons shows how short human history is.
But also how big and cool the Tyrannosaurus, iguanodons, etc were.
Jurassic Park was a brilliant movie, best one I remember from my childhood.

My second favourite museum is the science museum.
They have a hall full of rockets, need I elaborate?
I remember seeing the special exhibition for the Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.
Replica spaceships are almost as good as the real thing.
Plus it's a brilliant book!

[^1]: Replaced with the skeleton of a whale, so you can't see it anymore.
[^2]: it now has a sequel! After 25 years
