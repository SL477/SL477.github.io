A very weird book!
To be expected from the author of the Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.
It's something of a cross between that and Doctor Who (I believe that this started life as a Doctor Who episode, but the BBC didn't want it).
If you like either of those things, then this is for you.

Dick Gently demonstrates the interconnectedness of all things as he sets about the case by doing whatever he wants to do and is one of the few characters who enjoys everything.

The only real connection with the TV series is that they both have characters called Dirk Gently in them.
